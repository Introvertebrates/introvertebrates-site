<h2>What was found</h2>
<p>
  Researchers described <em>Balticolasma wunderlichi</em>, a fossil harvestman preserved in amber from deposits in Ukraine and the Baltic region. The fossils are about 35 million years old.
</p>
<p>
  The discovery is especially notable because it is the first known fossil representative of the subfamily Ortholasmatinae, adding an important piece to the record of arachnid history.
</p>

<h2>Why it matters</h2>
<p>
  Although this is not spider-specific, it is still valuable arachnology. Fossils like this help researchers understand how arachnid groups were distributed in the past and how long certain lineages have existed.
</p>
<p>
  It is also a reminder that amber fossils are still giving researchers new information rather than just confirming what was already known.
</p>
