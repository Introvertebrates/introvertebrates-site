Amber fossils reveal a strange arachnid from 35 million years ago

Researchers have described Balticolasma wunderlichi, a harvestman known from two 35-million-year-old amber fossils from Ukraine and the Baltic region. The find is notable because it represents the first fossil example ever reported from the subfamily Ortholasmatinae.

What was found

Researchers described Balticolasma wunderlichi, a fossil harvestman preserved in amber from deposits in Ukraine and the Baltic region. The fossils are about 35 million years old.

The discovery is especially notable because it is the first known fossil representative of the subfamily Ortholasmatinae, adding an important piece to the record of arachnid history.

Why it matters

Although this is not spider-specific, it is still valuable arachnology. Fossils like this help researchers understand how arachnid groups were distributed in the past and how long certain lineages have existed.

It is also a reminder that amber fossils are still giving researchers new information rather than just confirming what was already known.
